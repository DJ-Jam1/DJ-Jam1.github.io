Northern Barbarians: Mod Development, for Three Kingdoms

Northern Barbarians is a mod that I made in my spare time for Total War Three Kingdoms and is available on the Steam Workshop.
I led the Design and Developement from concept to launch on the Steam Workshop.

  • Link to Project

My Role included:

  • Full design of factions their mechanics and effects.
  • Scripting of missions/events/dilemmas and progression.
  • Design of characters, items and their effects.
  • Design for unique faction buildings and effects.
  • Design of unique faction battle units and balancing

My Design Process

Ideation

During Ideation my main focus was thinking about what people wanted/expected from this type of project.
From here then answering several questions and ultimatly creating the Pillars for the project to follow (see below).

  1. Life of the nomad everything must be useful nothing is wasted
  2. Fear I must make my enemies fear me to get what I want
  3. Opportunity The timing must be perfect or I risk losing everything

Research & Documentation

Research started on the Wuhuan Tribe and key characters first.


This was done because there was a large amount of historical texts relating to the tribe around the Three Kingdoms era and interactions with other key players of the time.
This important decision lead to:

  • Great Opportunities to flesh out the faction/characters and make it more unique due to the larger amount of historical texts available.
  • Allowed to create a strong and cohesive narrative based around historical events and the characters in them.
  • All of this was important as it helped to shape the overall player experience and made it more believable.

Prototyping & Feedback

After intial designs had been created on paper they were implemented in-game using the modding tools availbale through Steam.
From this point the core experience/features were added and I conducted small playtests as I added in features and made small tweaks when needed based on playtesting.


After all features where in-game and in a state that I was happy with I released the project on the Steam Workshop for a larger playtest and to gather feedback from my targte audience.
Using this iterative approach to this project gave me several benefits:

  1. Allowed for faster turnaround between iterations
  2. Focussed the work on the core experience avoiding feature creep
  3. Gave access to immediate feedback from target audience
